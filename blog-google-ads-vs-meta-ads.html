Google Ads vs. Meta Ads: Where to Spend?

VSS Performance Team

Jan 12, 2026 • 6 min read

One of the most common questions we get from founders at VSS is: "Should I be spending my budget on Google or Meta?" The answer, as you might expect, is "it depends." But let's look at the data to understand why. This choice is critical for your Performance Marketing strategy.

The Intent Gap

The primary difference between the two platforms is intent. Understanding this is key to reducing your CAC, as seen in our Fintech Case Study.

  • Google Ads (Search): High intent. Users are actively looking for a solution. They have a problem and they want it solved now.
  • Meta Ads (Social): Proactive discovery. Users aren't looking for you; you're interrupting their feed with something they might find interesting.

When to Choose Google Ads

Google Ads is usually the winner for B2B services, emergency services, and high-ticket items where search volume already exists. It's about capturing existing demand.

When to Choose Meta Ads

Meta is incredible for brand building and visual products. If you're using AI to scale your content, Meta is a great place to distribute it and build awareness.

The Hybrid Approach

The most successful brands use both. Use Meta to create awareness, then use Google Search to capture them. To maximize your return, make sure your landing pages are optimized.
