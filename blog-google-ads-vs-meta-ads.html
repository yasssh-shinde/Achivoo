Google Ads vs. Meta Ads: Where to Spend?

VSS Performance Team

Jan 12, 2026 • 6 min read

One of the most common questions we get from founders is: "Should I be spending my budget on Google or Meta?" The answer, as you might expect, is "it depends." But let's look at the data to understand why.

The Intent Gap

The primary difference between the two platforms is intent.

  • Google Ads (Search): High intent. Users are actively looking for a solution. They have a problem and they want it solved now.
  • Meta Ads (Social): Proactive discovery. Users aren't looking for you; you're interrupting their feed with something they might find interesting.

When to Choose Google Ads

Google Ads is usually the winner for B2B services, emergency services (plumbers, locksmiths), and high-ticket items where the search volume already exists.

When to Choose Meta Ads

Meta is incredible for brand building, visual products (D2C), and creating demand where none currently exists. If you have a product that people didn't know they needed, Meta's visual storytelling is your best friend.

The Hybrid Approach

The most successful brands use both. Use Meta to create awareness and "warm up" the audience, then use Google Search and Retargeting to capture them when they're ready to buy.
