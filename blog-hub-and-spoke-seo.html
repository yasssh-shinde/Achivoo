The "Hub & Spoke" SEO Model Explained

VSS Strategy Team

Jan 13, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

In the ever-evolving landscape of search engine optimization, one strategy consistently stands out for its ability to build authority and dominate competitive niches: the Hub and Spoke model.

What is the Hub & Spoke Model?

Also known as content clusters or topic clusters, this model is a method of organizing your website's content to help search engines understand your topical authority. It consists of two main components:

  • The Hub (Pillar Page): A comprehensive, high-level guide on a broad topic.
  • The Spokes (Cluster Content): Multiple, more specific articles that dive deep into sub-topics related to the Hub.

Why It Works

Search engines like Google have moved away from focusing solely on individual keywords. Instead, they look for "authority" on a subject. When you link multiple relevant "spoke" articles to a central "hub," you're signaling to Google that your site is a deep resource for that entire topic.

"Authority is not built by a single page, but by a network of high-quality content that solves the user's entire journey."

How to Implement It

  1. Identify your Core Topic: Choose a broad topic that is central to your business.
  2. Perform Keyword Research: Find the long-tail questions and specific sub-topics people search for.
  3. Create the Hub: Write a 2,000+ word comprehensive guide.
  4. Develop the Spokes: Create 5-10 specific articles that each focus on one sub-topic.
  5. Link them strategically: All spokes must link to the hub, and the hub should link out to all spokes.

Conclusion

The Hub and Spoke model is a long-term play, but its results are undeniable. By building a network of content, you create a "moat" around your rankings that competitors will find hard to cross.
